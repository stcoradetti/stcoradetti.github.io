Adventures with Presto

We recently lost our beloved Presto, snuggle feind and hiker extraorinaire. To chase the blues away I thought I'd gather some of my favorite pictures of him on adventures with us and make some notes for posterity. It turns out there were a lot of them. Enjoy.


June 2014 - Bolinas Ridge Trai in Marin County, CA
Lori and I had just started dating. She was pretty new to California and I didn't have much experience hiking with dogs out there. I was pretty taken aback to find out the marquee state and national parks banned all dogs on trails. It seemed at first that we would be limited to pretty lame trails like this one, a long slow ramp up a dead-grass ridge under the baking sun. We would find though that there were quite a lot of awesome places we could still go. Always taking Presto meant we ended up going a ton of less well-known places where we enjoyed some spectacular scenery, usually with zero crowds whatsoever.

July 2014 - The Lost Coast (0 ft), north of Fort Bragg, CA
Our first backpacking adventure with Presto was on 'The Lost Coast' of Northern Califorina, a remote 30-mile stretch where 4,000 foot mountains drop right to the beach and you have to time your hike to the tide table. It's 'Lost' becuase it was too rugged to build a road there. Seems like 'Saved Coast' would be better name for it. Jim Craig and Wilfried Jonkers came along, and we had a grand fourth of July weekend isolated from the entire world except a few parties of extremely adventurous surfers.

We tried getting Presto to carry some of his own gear on this trip, but he really did not enjoy it. He would get free sherpa services from here on out. Keeping the booties refreshed was pretty critical on the rough, rocky sand on this stretch. A few sections had awkward rocks he need to be carried over, but mostly he did fine.

July 2014 - Levitt Peak (11569 ft), near Sonora Pass, CA
We knew that lots of national forest land was open for dogs, so soon after we started to dispair of our options around the SF Bay area, we went straight for the Sierra. This was the highest peak I'd summited by over a thousand feet and I barely made the summit. Presto seemed unbothered. We know he was working hard though because his heartbeat was very regular. Usually, whippet hearts just have beat slowly and randomly unless they're running. It's like their oxygen delivery is so high they don't even have to regulate it when they're not running around.

Presto was so nervous to be left alone on the shore he came out on the log to shiver next to Lori.

July 2016 - Dicks Peak (9,974 ft), near Lake Tahoe, CA
Here we have Presto napping on a precarious hillside in the middle of an epic 16 mile day hike to the summit of Dick's Peak in the Desolation Wilderness. We were waiting for another pair of hikers to come down a rocky slope and trying to stay out of the way of any boulders they might dislodge, so we were standing in a fairly awkward spot to keep protected from said boulders. Presto decided this was a fine spot for a nap. This was the genesis of the P-index. P = T * C, where T is the time in seconds we've stopped making any forward progress before Presto decides to just plonk down right there and take a nap, and C is the comfy-ness of the ground on a scale of 1 to 10. The lower the P-index, the harder the hike. A light hike is around P = 1000. This hike was P = 10.

Everyone still made the peak though. Here he is tackling the final rock scramble.
